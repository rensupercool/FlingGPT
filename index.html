Core loop

Grab, control, break rhythm, finish low.

FTAP PvP is built by players around pallet control, timing, aim, and momentum. There are no built-in dash or bunny-hop systems for combat movement.

Grab reach

30 studs

Aiming is proximity based, so timing and distance matter as much as crosshair placement.

Foundation

Core mechanics that decide fights.

FTAP combat is less about a formal moveset and more about how players use the pallet, reach limit, velocity, and timing windows.

Flinging

Grab the opponent, move them into the floor to trigger ragdoll more consistently, then release low enough that clutching becomes harder.

Ragdolling

Usually happens when someone is grabbed at decent speed, especially in air-to-air fights. It limits recovery and makes flings more threatening.

Aiming

It feels like FPS aim, but players move fast enough that prediction and timing become just as important as tracking.

Movement

Movement comes from grabbing the pallet at different positions, scrolling for speed, diving by looking down, or dragging back and upward for control.

AG / anti-grab

AG is a distance check that cancels grabs when speed, distance, or ping makes the grab fail validation. Good slow players can bait rushes into AG.

Clutching

Recovery after being flung is heavily ping based. Low flings and dunking reduce clutch chances; tabbing is frowned upon as an exploit.

Rank ladder

How FTAP skill is commonly labeled.

The supplied hierarchy runs from casual to fling god. Skillful and above commonly use I, II, and III tiers, plus low, mid, and high modifiers.

1

Casual

Grounded most of the time, can fling, can barely fly, and usually struggles with aim.

2

Apprentice / app

Can fly and grab straightforward face-to-face opportunities. Uses low, mid, and high labels.

3

Skillful / sk

Where players start getting good. Skillful players can fly without falling, grab easy chances consistently, and juke with fast direction changes.

4

Expert / exp

Often the best in a server. Experts are usually very strong at movement, aiming, or both.

5

Advanced / adv

Very rare. Advanced players are usually known, exceptional, and strong in both movement and aim.

6

Fling god / fg

No tiers, only levels. Reserved for the most notable players at their peaks.

Formats and map

PvP is community-made.

The notes describe no official PvP modes. Players organize fights in public lobbies or through private 1v1s, 2v2s, 3v3s, and FFAs.

Map cadence

The map mostly stays the same. Seasonal maps may appear, often from older prepared map sets.

Pallet throwing

Throwing is high risk because the pallet can go too far or get AG. It is semi-unheard-of as a technique.

Clutch counters

Low fling or dunk.

Low flings make recovery harder. Dunking throws downward and removes most clutch options except frowned-upon tabbing or unusual item saves.

Ranking history

Community ranked.

Fling Enthusiasts and Fling Syndicate are described as older ranking hubs. Current ranking is mostly handled by known high-level players.

Playstyles

Fast pressure or slow control.

Most players begin from basic center-mass pallet movement, then branch toward fast rushes, slow baiting, or a hybrid of both.

Fast archetypes

rhythm pressure

High FPS

Very high FPS increases speed and control, enabling frequent dives and rapid stops.

Aggressive-fast

Rush-heavy players who rely on pressure, decent aim, and fewer fancy transitions.

Controlled-fast

Fast movement that stays readable to the player using it, allowing tech to flow naturally.

Slow archetypes

reaction control

Very slow-slow

Waits for rushes, then dashes back or dives to punish the approach.

Hybrid-slow

Usually not fully slow, but shifts slower when reacting to a bad rush or risky move.

Elite slow

Baits AG, dives under approaches, and punishes timing. Beating it requires baiting the dive before committing.

Advanced tech

Pallet methods, grabs, resets, and rhythm breaks.

CM means cool method in the source notes. Percentages below come from those notes and describe the stated possibility, practicality, or chemistry.

Stutter-step

fast

A rhythm break that stops most speed by looking down and resetting on the pallet. Best with fast playstyles because it interrupts predictable rush timing.

CM possibility 90%, practicality 80%.

Pallet resetting

reset

A fast direction-switching reset done around the pallet. It can slightly shift elevation and pairs well with instant diving and stutter-step chains.

CM possibility 70%, practicality 80%.

Instant grabbing

grab

A close timing grab that works best for spin-heavy and tracking-heavy styles. It is strongest when movement does the aiming and the input lands at the right tick.

CM possibility 90%, practicality up to 100% on fitting styles.

AG bypassing

experimental

An unexplored triple-grab concept meant to raise the chance of securing a grab through anti-grab checks. The notes describe it as heavily chance-based.

CM possibility 20%, practicality 40%.

Pallet tapping

mindgame

A fake early rush where the pallet is tapped mid-approach to pause for a variable moment. Useful because the delay can be short or long enough to break reads.

CM possibility 80%, practicality 60%.

Pallet flicking

flick

A grab attempt while still holding the pallet and flicking quickly. It favors slower speeds because misses are less punishing and small flicks are more consistent.

Small-flick possibility 70%, CM practicality 60%.

Insta diving

dive

A dive chained into a pallet reset so momentum is refreshed and the player can rush immediately after dropping elevation.

CM possibility 100%, practicality 80%.

Scroll faking

unconfirmed

An unconfirmed visual desync where an opponent may see max scroll in one direction while the player is not actually moving that way.

Possibility around 10%; practical only if consistent.

Throw resetting

fallback

A weaker reset using the throw mechanic. It changes elevation slightly and works best with spin styles that keep the pallet visible.

Possible 70%, practical 10%.

Stunning

pressure

A disruption where the opponent's pallet is grabbed so they have to look down and recover, creating a free grab window.

Possible 100%, practical around 70% with CM.

Player clutching

survival

A clutch that uses the opponent's body while both players are grabbing each other. It favors slower survival-heavy play because those players get grabbed more often.

The notes describe mastered consistency as very high.

Pre-strafing

control

Strafing before the turn or spin you want to do. Fast and spin playstyles use it to add control to actions that may otherwise fail.

Best with fast and spin playstyles.
