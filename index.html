Core mechanics

How the game actually works

None of this is "built in" — flinging, movement and clutching were all discovered and refined by players. Aiming is proximity based with a hard 30-stud reach.

🎯

Movement

Think of a Garry's Mod physics gun aimed at your pallet — it has a 30-stud range, which doubles as your grab range. Scroll out to fly faster and grab further back (less control), or grab high and scroll less for tight control. Looking down and scrolling out is the dive. There is no built-in dashing or bunny-hopping — every bit of movement is player-made.

⛔

AG — Anti-Grab

A distance check written by the dev horomori to kill infinite-reach exploits. When someone moves too fast on high ping, the grab gets cancelled — brutal after you fought for it. It triggers on dash-backs and dives into a rush, and it's exactly why rupasu is so deadly: rushing him hands you an AG while he calmly stops and grabs you.

Survival

Clutching & counters

FTAP's most unfair mechanic. It's heavily ping-based — the lower your ping, the more outs you have when you get flung.

The ladder

Rank hierarchy

From flightless casuals to the untouchable fling gods. Most ranks use tiers I–III (1-2-3), each further split low / mid / high. Casual is the most common rank overall; skillful is the most common for competitive players.

Techniques & chaining

The real depth of FTAP. "CM" = the Cool Method — most of these become viable, consistent, or downright unfair once it's in play. Each card rates possibility (can it be done) and practicality (is it worth doing) with and without CM.

How to beat a slow player

You can't rush them straight up — that's exactly what they want. Bait the dive, rush in, make them commit to the dodge, then you stop rushing and dive yourself to break their timing and grab them while they reset. Against rupasu specifically, even this barely works.
